## Dimension 4: Domain & Industry Knowledge

This dimension encompasses the specialized knowledge of specific scientific, engineering, or business domains where HPC and IT infrastructure are applied. These skills are about understanding the **workloads, use cases, and regulatory contexts** that drive the need for high-performance computing.

Here are the 10 top categories of Domain & Industry Knowledge skills:

---

### 1. Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
The understanding of AI/ML workflows, frameworks, and use cases that increasingly drive HPC infrastructure demands.

- Deep Learning frameworks: PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX
- Large Language Models (LLMs) and Generative AI
- Distributed training strategies (DeepSpeed, Megatron-LM, Horovod, Ray)
- Model inference and serving (vLLM, TensorRT-LLM, Triton)
- AI/ML pipelines and MLOps (MLflow, Kubeflow)
- Foundation models and fine-tuning (LoRA, parameter-efficient tuning)
- AI for Science (scientific machine learning)
- Computer vision and NLP

---

### 2. Scientific Computing & Research Domains
Knowledge of specific scientific disciplines and their computational requirements.

- Computational Physics and Chemistry
- Bioinformatics and Genomics
- Climate and Weather Modeling (WRF, UM)
- Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) – OpenFOAM, ANSYS Fluent
- Finite Element Analysis (FEA) – Altair, OptiStruct, Radioss
- Molecular Dynamics and Atomistic Simulations
- Digital Biology and Drug Discovery
- Material Science and Nanotechnology
- Seismic Data Processing and Reservoir Simulation (Oil & Gas)

---

### 3. Electronic Design Automation (EDA) & Semiconductor
Understanding of the workflows and tools used in chip design and semiconductor manufacturing.

- EDA tools and workflows (Cadence, Synopsys, Siemens EDA)
- Semiconductor design and manufacturing processes
- Chip simulation and verification workloads
- Physical design and layout
- ASIC and FPGA design flows
- EDA-specific job scheduling and licensing
- Semiconductor industry standards and practices

---

### 4. Engineering & Manufacturing
Knowledge of engineering simulation and manufacturing applications.

- Computer-Aided Engineering (CAE) software
- Product design and simulation
- Automotive and Aerospace engineering
- Structural analysis and multi-physics simulation
- Thermal and electromotive systems
- Manufacturing process simulation
- Virtual engineering and digital twins

---

### 5. Defense, National Security & Government
Understanding of security classifications, compliance frameworks, and mission requirements for government and defense organizations.

- Classified environment operations (SCI, SAP, Top Secret)
- Department of Defense (DoD) and Intelligence Community (IC) standards
- Federal security frameworks (NIST, FISMA, FedRAMP, DISA STIGs)
- Government procurement and acquisition processes
- National lab and research center operations
- Defense mission applications
- Security clearance and special access programs

---

### 6. Financial Services & Quantitative Finance
Knowledge of computational finance and high-frequency trading environments.

- Quantitative research and modeling
- High-Frequency Trading (HFT) infrastructure
- Risk analysis and financial simulations
- Market data processing and analytics
- Algorithmic trading systems
- Low-latency compute requirements
- Regulatory compliance in financial services

---

### 7. Life Sciences & Healthcare
Understanding of biomedical research, clinical data, and healthcare applications.

- Biomedical research and imaging (MRI, fMRI, neuroimaging)
- Genomics and next-generation sequencing (NGS)
- Clinical data management and HIPAA compliance
- Bioinformatics pipelines (e.g., Nextflow)
- Drug discovery and computational biology
- Healthcare AI and medical imaging
- Research computing in hospital/university settings

---

### 8. Energy & Utilities
Knowledge of energy exploration, production, and distribution applications.

- Oil and Gas subsurface applications
- Reservoir simulation and seismic data processing
- Renewable energy modeling
- Power grid simulation and optimization
- Nuclear energy research
- Environmental sustainability and carbon modeling
- Energy efficiency and green computing

---

### 9. Research & Academic Environments
Understanding of the specific needs and constraints of academic and research institutions.

- University and national lab research computing
- Multi-tenant, multi-user environments with diverse workloads
- Grant writing and research proposal support
- Teaching and training in computational methods
- Academic user support and onboarding
- Open-source software adoption and contribution
- Collaborative scientific research across disciplines

---

### 10. Business & Strategic Acumen
Understanding of business operations, strategy, and decision-making in HPC contexts.

- Capacity planning and cost-benefit analysis
- Vendor management and technology procurement
- Technology roadmap development
- Business development and partnership-building
- Market analysis and competitive intelligence
- Executive-level communication and influence
- Proposal and contract development (RFI/RFP response)
- Return on Investment (ROI) analysis for infrastructure investments

---

This fourth dimension represents the **"what" and "why"** that sits on top of the **"how"** of IT and HPC skills. It positions the engineer not just as a technology operator, but as a domain expert who understands the business, scientific, or mission context that makes the technology valuable.
