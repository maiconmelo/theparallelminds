Based on a thorough analysis of the job descriptions, there are **four distinct levels of exigence** (requirement) used throughout the positions. Your assessment is largely correct, but with some nuance in how they are defined and applied.

Here are the four levels with their precise definitions and examples from the text:

---

## The Four Levels of Exigence

| Level | Typical Wording | Meaning | Candidate Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Required** | "Required", "Must have", "Basic Qualifications", "Essential", "Minimum" | Non-negotiable. The candidate **must** possess this skill or qualification to be considered. The role cannot be performed without it. | **Deal-breaker** if missing. |
| **2. Preferred** | "Preferred", "Preferred Qualifications", "Strongly Preferred", "Highly Desirable", "Nice to have" | Highly valued but not mandatory. Candidates with these skills will be prioritized, but those without can still be considered if they excel in other areas. | **Strong advantage**; can differentiate candidates. |
| **3. Desirable** | "Desirable", "Will be considered a plus", "Advantageous", "Valuable", "Also looking for" | Beneficial but lower priority than "Preferred." These are skills that would be useful but are not critical to the role's success. | **Bonus point**; helpful but not decisive. |
| **4. Bonus / Stand Out** | "Ways to Stand Out", "Stand Out From The Crowd", "Bonus", "Differentiator" | Exceptional or differentiating skills that are not expected but would make a candidate truly remarkable. Often includes advanced expertise, contributions to open source, or unique combinations of skills. | **Competitive differentiator**; can secure an offer over equally qualified candidates. |

---

## Detailed Analysis with Examples from the Job Postings

### 1. Required (Non-Negotiable)

These are the **minimum qualifications** without which a candidate would be immediately rejected. They are often found in sections titled "Basic Qualifications," "Requirements," or "What We Need To See."

**Typical Wording:**
- "Required"
- "Must have"
- "Basic Qualifications"
- "Essential"
- "Minimum"
- "You must have"

**Examples:**

> *"Deep hands-on Slurm experience (installation, configuration, scheduling policies, accounting, multi-cluster federation)"* – **Required** for the HPC Engineer - AI Infrastructure (GPU Cloud) role.

> *"Active TS/SCI clearance is required"* – **Required** for many defense and intelligence roles.

> *"Strong Linux systems administration experience (core requirement)"* – **Required** for the Mistral AI Systems Engineer role.

> *"Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field"* – **Required** for many roles (though sometimes experience can substitute).

**Impact:** If a candidate does not meet a **Required** skill, they are typically not considered further, regardless of other strengths.

---

### 2. Preferred (Highly Valued)

These skills are **strongly desired** and will significantly boost a candidate's chances. They are often found in sections titled "Preferred Qualifications" or "What We Need To See" (when listed alongside requirements with nuance).

**Typical Wording:**
- "Preferred"
- "Preferred Qualifications"
- "Strongly preferred"
- "Highly desirable"
- "What we are looking for" (when used in a prioritized list)
- "Nice to have" (when used in a "Required" section but distinguished as secondary)

**Examples:**

> *"Experience with Lustre is strongly preferred"* – **Preferred** for the HPC Infrastructure & Scheduler Engineer (PBS Professional) role.

> *"AWS ParallelCluster or PCS experience is a plus"* – While worded as a "plus," this sits in the **Preferred** category as it is mentioned directly in the "Key areas" list without the "required" framing.

> *"Experience with GPU clusters is preferred"* – **Preferred** for the HPC Engineer - Remote (1 Year Experience) role.

> *"NVIDIA AI Infrastructure / DGX / AI Factory certifications are strongly preferred"* – **Strongly Preferred** for the Project Director -- HPC / AI Infrastructure Deployment role.

**Impact:** Candidates who meet all **Required** skills and many **Preferred** skills will be at the top of the shortlist. Missing a Preferred skill is rarely fatal.

---

### 3. Desirable (Beneficial but Lower Priority)

These skills are **helpful** but not central to the role. They are often listed in sections titled "Desirable," "Nice to Have" (when clearly separated from core requirements), or as "Additional experience that may be helpful."

**Typical Wording:**
- "Desirable"
- "Nice-to-have"
- "Advantageous"
- "Will be considered a plus"
- "Additional experience that may be helpful"
- "Valuable"
- "Also looking for"

**Examples:**

> *"Exposure to cloud platforms and hybrid HPC models"* – **Desirable** for the HPC Infrastructure & Scheduler Engineer role (listed as "Exposure" without strong emphasis).

> *"Familiarity with containerization technologies (Docker, Kubernetes) is a plus"* – **Desirable** for the HPC Engineer - Honeywell role.

> *"Exposure to AI/ML infrastructure and data analytics platforms is highly desirable"* – **Desirable** for the Technical Leader -- HPC & Simulation Environments (German Speaking) role.

> *"Experience with OpenStack is advantageous"* – **Desirable** for the HPC Technologist - Hybrid HPC Environment role.

> *"Knowledge of IT security policies, data encryption, and access control management"* – Listed as **Preferred Qualifications** but with wording like "Familiarity" and "Knowledge," placing it in the **Desirable** tier.

**Impact:** These skills are nice to have but will rarely influence a hiring decision unless two candidates are perfectly matched on all other criteria.

---

### 4. Bonus / Stand Out (Differentiators)

These are the **exceptional skills** that make a candidate truly remarkable. They are often found in sections titled "Ways to Stand Out From The Crowd," "Stand Out From The Crowd," or "Bonus." These are not expected but represent the ideal candidate.

**Typical Wording:**
- "Ways to Stand Out From The Crowd"
- "Stand Out From The Crowd"
- "Bonus"
- "Differentiator"
- "A strong plus"

**Examples:**

> *"Contributions to open-source projects in the Kubernetes or NVIDIA ecosystem"* – **Bonus** for the HPC Solution Architect - Kubernetes role.

> *"Industry recognized leader in HPC/DL communications with history of patents, publications and conference talks"* – **Stand Out** for the Distinguished Software Architect - Deep Learning and HPC Communications role at NVIDIA.

> *"Experience with AI-assisted workflows, AI coding assistants, and custom model development"* – **Bonus** for the Staff HPC Engineer role.

> *"Maintainer or co‑maintainer responsibilities for an open source component used in production (plugins, operators, exporters, controllers, or SDKs) at large scale"* – **Stand Out** for the Senior Software Engineer - HPC role at NVIDIA.

> *"Experience designing, deploying AI workflow technologies such as Apache Airflow, Prefect, Dagster"* – Listed as "Ways to stand out from the crowd" for the Senior HPC and AI Cluster Administrator - Accenture Federal Services role.

**Impact:** These skills are not required and rarely expected. However, candidates who possess them can leapfrog others who have similar levels of required and preferred skills.

| **Required** | "Required", "Must have", "Essential", "Minimum", "Basic Qualifications", "Core", "Fundamental" | Basic Qualifications, Requirements, What We Need To See (core list) |
| **Preferred** | "Preferred", "Strongly preferred", "Highly desirable", "What we're looking for" | Preferred Qualifications, Additional Requirements, What We Need To See (secondary list) |
| **Desirable** | "Desirable", "Nice to have", "Plus", "Advantageous", "Valuable", "A bonus", "Helpful", "Exposure to", "Familiarity with" | Desirable Skills, Nice-to-Have, Additional Experience |

---

## Practical Advice for Candidates

| Your Situation | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Missing a Required skill** | Do not apply unless you can demonstrate equivalent experience or a very strong justification. Focus on roles where you meet all Required skills. |
| **Missing some Preferred skills** | Apply confidently if you meet all Required skills. Emphasize your strengths in other Preferred and Domain areas. |
| **Missing all Desirable skills** | This is normal. Desirable skills are often a wishlist. Apply if you meet Required + most Preferred. |
| **Possessing Bonus skills** | Highlight these prominently in your resume and cover letter. They can differentiate you even if you lack some Preferred skills. |
| **Unsure about a skill** | If a skill is listed as "Preferred" or "Desirable" but you have related experience, mention it. Employers often use these as tie-breakers, not absolute filters. |

---

## The Missing Fifth Level: "Nice to Have" vs. "Bonus"

You might notice some overlap between **Desirable** and **Bonus**. The distinction is:

- **Desirable**: Skills that would be useful but are not differentiating. Example: *"Familiarity with Docker"* for a general HPC role.
- **Bonus**: Skills that are genuinely impressive and rare. Example: *"Maintainer of a major open-source HPC project"* for a senior engineer role.

In practice, many job postings use these terms interchangeably. The key is to understand that **the further down the list you go, the less critical the skill is to the role.**
