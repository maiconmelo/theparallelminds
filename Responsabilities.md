## Detailed Breakdown with Examples

### 1. Design & Architecture

**Definition:** Planning, designing, and evolving the infrastructure, systems, and solutions to meet current and future requirements. This includes hardware selection, capacity planning, and architectural decision-making.

**Typical Wording:** *"Design," "Architect," "Plan," "Develop architecture," "Define standards," "Evaluate technologies," "Create roadmaps"*

**Examples:**

> *"Design and implement HPC clusters, including compute, storage, networking, and job‑scheduling components"* – Principal HPC Architect - KLA

> *"Lead the architecture, design, and lifecycle management of on‑prem, hybrid, and cloud‑based HPC platforms"* – Technical Business Analyst (HPC, Linux) - DSM-H Consulting

> *"Design next-generation AI and HPC infrastructure that utilizes advanced memory solutions to optimize performance and power efficiency"* – AI/HPC System Architect - SK hynix America

> *"Architect and optimize environments for large-scale AI training and tuning, and low-latency scientific workloads"* – Staff HPC Engineer

---

### 2. Deployment & Provisioning

**Definition:** Installing, configuring, and bringing online new hardware, software, and infrastructure components. This includes bare-metal provisioning, OS installation, and cluster bring-up.

**Typical Wording:** *"Deploy," "Install," "Provision," "Configure," "Bring up," "Rack and stack," "Image," "Onboard"*

**Examples:**

> *"Deploy, configure, and maintain large-scale Linux-based HPC clusters comprising CPU and GPU nodes, high-speed interconnects, and parallel file systems"* – Sr. HPC Systems Engineer - Johns Hopkins University

> *"Plan and build complex cluster and supercomputers in various data centers and labs; rack stack and cable management"* – Data Center Engineer, HPC and AI - NVIDIA (Israel)

> *"Design and implement bare-metal provisioning workflows using Ironic and Kubernetes CRDs"* – Senior HPC Infrastructure Engineer - Firmus Technologies

> *"Image, configure, and upgrade servers with Linux operating systems, including firmware updates and switch configuration"* – Senior Linux System Administrator - HPE

---

### 3. Operations & Maintenance

**Definition:** Day-to-day management, upkeep, and troubleshooting of production systems. This includes patching, upgrades, hardware repair, incident response, and ensuring system availability and reliability.

**Typical Wording:** *"Operate," "Maintain," "Support," "Troubleshoot," "Administer," "Patch," "Upgrade," "Incident response," "On-call"*

**Examples:**

> *"Operate and maintain a large-scale Linux-based HPC cluster used for internal R&D workloads"* – HPC Systems Engineer - KLA (Ann Arbor, MI)

> *"Troubleshoot and resolve HPC cluster issues working closely with physical deployment teams on-site"* – HPC Operations Engineer - Lambda

> *"Install, configure, and maintain HPC systems, including compute, storage, and management nodes"* – HPC Systems Engineer - HPE

> *"Troubleshoot job failures, scheduler errors, resource constraints, and performance concerns, driving issues to resolution"* – HPC Operations Engineer - NVIDIA (India)

> *"Perform hardware maintenance, software installations and upgrades, inclusive of operating system"* – HPC Systems Engineer

---

### 4. Performance Tuning & Optimization

**Definition:** Identifying and resolving performance bottlenecks across compute, storage, and networking layers. Profiling applications, tuning system configurations, and optimizing resource utilization.

**Typical Wording:** *"Optimize," "Tune," "Profile," "Benchmark," "Analyze performance," "Identify bottlenecks," "Improve efficiency," "Benchmarking"*

**Examples:**

> *"Optimize NCCL configuration, collective communication patterns and multi-node interconnect performance across high-speed Ethernet and InfiniBand"* – HPC Engineer - AI Infrastructure (GPU Cloud)

> *"Profile and tune applications for CPU, GPU, memory, and I/O performance; optimize MPI, OpenMP, CUDA, and other parallel programming frameworks"* – Principal HPC Architect - KLA

> *"Design and execute performance benchmarking workloads, including MLPerf, NCCL tests, microbenchmarks, and throughput/latency validation"* – Senior HPC Infrastructure Engineer - Firmus Technologies

> *"Analyze and tune performance for a variety of EDA workloads; identify bottlenecks and implement scalable solutions"* – HPC Cluster Engineer - NVIDIA (EDA)

---

### 5. Monitoring & Observability

**Definition:** Implementing and maintaining systems to collect, visualize, and analyze metrics, logs, and traces. Ensuring visibility into system health, performance, and capacity.

**Typical Wording:** *"Monitor," "Observe," "Telemetry," "Dashboards," "Alerting," "Metrics," "Logs," "Prometheus," "Grafana," "Splunk"*

**Examples:**

> *"Build monitoring and profiling for HPC workloads: GPU utilization, memory bandwidth, interconnect throughput, job performance analytics"* – HPC Engineer - AI Infrastructure (GPU Cloud)

> *"Design and scale observability platforms handling high-volume metrics, logs, and traces across distributed environments"* – Senior AI and HPC Observability Engineer - NVIDIA

> *"Monitor system health, performance, and capacity; respond to incidents and degradations"* – HPC Systems Engineer - KLA (Ann Arbor, MI)

> *"Develop and implement robust monitoring tools to track resource utilization and identify performance bottlenecks"* – HPC Systems Administrator - Mass General Brigham

---

### 6. Automation & Tooling

**Definition:** Developing scripts, tools, and Infrastructure-as-Code to automate repetitive tasks, improve consistency, and reduce manual effort. This includes CI/CD pipelines, configuration management, and provisioning automation.

**Typical Wording:** *"Automate," "Script," "Tooling," "CI/CD," "Infrastructure as Code," "Ansible," "Terraform," "Python," "Bash," "GitOps"*

**Examples:**

> *"Build automation and improve runbooks, dashboards, alerts, and lifecycle workflows"* – HPC Engineer - CoreWeave

> *"Develop and maintain automation and monitoring frameworks for system provisioning, metrics collection, and alerting"* – Sr. HPC Systems Engineer - Johns Hopkins University

> *"Design and implement automation scripts (Bash, Python, Ansible) for routine cluster management, provisioning, user onboarding, and data synchronization tasks"* – Platform Engineer (ML Infrastructure) - TANUH

> *"Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines (GitLab CI / GitHub Actions) for infrastructure provisioning and HPC software delivery"* – Senior Infrastructure HPC Engineer

---

### 7. User Support & Enablement

**Definition:** Providing technical assistance, guidance, and training to end-users (researchers, scientists, engineers, developers). Helping users run their workloads efficiently, troubleshooting issues, and onboarding new users.

**Typical Wording:** *"Support users," "Assist researchers," "User support," "Training," "Onboarding," "Help desk," "Ticketing," "Triage," "Consulting"*

**Examples:**

> *"Provide technical support and guidance to researchers and staff using the HPC cluster for computational tasks"* – HPC Systems Administrator - Mass General Brigham

> *"Support researchers with job optimization, GPU utilization best practices, and performance tuning for AI and HPC applications"* – Staff HPC Engineer

> *"Act as the primary technical interface between infrastructure operations and internal or external users running AI workflows"* – HPC / AI Specialist - AI4I (Turin, Italy)

> *"Respond to and resolve HPC user support tickets; assist users with job submission, scheduling, and workflow optimization"* – HPC User Support Specialist - GRNET

---

### 8. Security & Compliance

**Definition:** Ensuring systems are secure, hardened, and compliant with organizational, government, or industry regulations. Includes access control, vulnerability management, patching, auditing, and working with security teams.

**Typical Wording:** *"Security," "Compliance," "Harden," "Patch," "Access control," "RBAC," "IAM," "STIG," "NIST," "Audit," "Clearance"*

**Examples:**

> *"Ensure all HPC systems comply with security policies and regulatory requirements, implementing necessary controls and conducting regular audits"* – Senior HPC Engineer (Classified) - ORNL

> *"Hardening, patching, and tuning Linux/UNIX/Windows systems; implement OS-level enhancements to improve reliability and performance"* – High-Performance Computing (HPC) (SA3) (Government) - AT&T

> *"Ensure security and compliance through configuration hardening, patch management, and integration with campus identity and access control systems"* – Sr. HPC Systems Engineer - Johns Hopkins University

> *"Support identity management, authentication, and access control frameworks to maintain secure and compliant environments"* – HPC Linux Systems Engineer, Classified Environment - ORNL

---

### 9. Documentation & Knowledge Transfer

**Definition:** Creating and maintaining clear, comprehensive documentation of architectures, configurations, procedures, runbooks, and troubleshooting guides. Training others and sharing knowledge.

**Typical Wording:** *"Document," "Documentation," "Runbooks," "Knowledge transfer," "Training," "User guides," "SOPs," "Procedures"*

**Examples:**

> *"Document system designs, architectures, procedures, and troubleshooting guides to support knowledge transfer and team continuity"* – Sr. HPC Systems Engineer - Johns Hopkins University

> *"Create and maintain detailed documentation of HPC architectures, configurations, and operational procedures"* – Senior HPC Engineer (Classified) - ORNL

> *"Produce clear documentation, diagrams, and reusable tooling that enable others to operate, debug, and extend the platform"* – Senior HPC Engineer - Millennium

> *"Develop user-facing documentation, maintain technical documentation, and contribute to knowledge sharing"* – HPC Engineer - Sabre (DoD)

---

### 10. Leadership, Strategy & Mentorship

**Definition:** Leading teams, projects, or initiatives. Mentoring junior staff, defining strategy, representing the team to leadership, and influencing technical direction. This is most common in senior, lead, and architect roles.

**Typical Wording:** *"Lead," "Mentor," "Strategy," "Roadmap," "Guide," "Influence," "Technical leadership," "Thought leadership," "Stakeholder management," "Presentations"*

**Examples:**

> *"Lead the design and deployment of HPC systems, ensuring they meet the computational needs and security requirements of a classified environment"* – Senior HPC Engineer (Classified) - ORNL

> *"Provide technical leadership, mentorship, and guidance to junior engineers; define architecture standards and design governance"* – Principal HPC Architect - KLA

> *"Mentor and cross-train existing system administrators, building the team's collective HPC expertise"* – HPC System Administrator - Santa Clara University

> *"Act as a thought leader in HPC infrastructure, capable of partnering with scientists and translating complex research needs into high-impact computing solutions"* – Staff HPC Engineer

> *"Lead HPC-related projects, from initial planning and design through to implementation and operational support"* – Senior HPC Engineer (Classified) - ORNL
